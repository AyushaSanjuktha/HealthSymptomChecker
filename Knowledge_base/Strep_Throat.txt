Condition: Strep Throat

Symptoms: Sudden sore throat, pain when swallowing, fever, red and swollen tonsils (sometimes with white patches or streaks of pus), tiny red spots on the roof of the mouth, and body aches.

Causes: A bacterial infection caused by Group A Streptococcus bacteria.

Prevention: Wash your hands often, avoid sharing food or drinks, and stay home if you have symptoms to prevent spreading the bacteria.

When to see a doctor: See a doctor for diagnosis and treatment. Strep throat requires antibiotics to prevent more serious complications.
